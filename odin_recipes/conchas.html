Conchas recipe

Description

Conchas are traditional Mexican sweet bread rolls (a.k.a. pan dulce). Its name comes from the roll’s seashell-like appearance. Conchas consist of a sweet bread base and crunchy sugar topping.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup warm water
  • 2 ½ teaspoons yeast
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup evaporated milk
  • ⅜ cup white sugar
  • ⅓ cup butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon

Steps

  1. To make the bread: Stir warm water and yeast together in a large bowl; let stand until yeast softens and begins to form a creamy foam, about 5 minutes
  2. Mix in 2 cups flour, milk, sugar, melted butter, egg, and salt until combined.
  3. Gradually mix in remaining 2 cups flour and cinnamon until dough comes together.
  4. Turn dough out onto a floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, 6 to 8 minutes. Place in a large greased bowl; turn dough to coat.
  5. Cover and let rise in a warm place until doubled, about 1 hour.
  6. Meanwhile, make the topping: Beat sugar and butter in a medium bowl with an electric mixer until light and fluffy.
  7. Stir in flour until a thick paste forms.
  8. Transfer 1/2 of the paste to a separate bowl. Mix cinnamon into one half and vanilla into the other half.
  9. Divide dough into 12 equal pieces. Shape into balls and place 3 inches apart on a greased cookie sheet.
  10. Divide each bowl of topping into 6 balls; pat flat. Place circles of topping on top of dough balls, patting down lightly. Use a knife to cut grooves in the topping like a seashell.
  11. Cover and let rise until doubled, about 45 minutes.
  12. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  13. Bake in the preheated oven until lightly golden brown, about 20 minutes.
